1. ADHD EXPERIENTIAL FRAMEWORK: DESIGNING FOR NEURODIVERGENCY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Children with Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) experience 
unique differences in neurochemistry (such as dopamine regulation) and 
cognitive processing. Traditional learning utilities often fail them due 
to monotonous structures and high delay of reinforcement. This game is 
architected from the ground up to support, engage, and help children with 
ADHD through several specific psychological design pillars:

A. Optimal Stimulation & Immediate Praise:
   ADHD is often characterized by a baseline deficit in tonic dopamine levels, 
   rendering standard educational interfaces dry or taxing. By offering 
   instant magnetic snapping, highly encouraging and colorful QML 
   transitions, and satisfying synthetic sound effects (triggered on-demand 
   via the C++ backend), the game supplies an optimal flow of micro-rewards. 
   These frequent positive reinforcements help maintain task-oriented arousal 
   without overwhelming the child's sensory threshold.

B. Mitigated Cognitive Load & Executive Scaffolding:
   ADHD directly impacts executive functions, including working memory and 
   organization. The clean, zero-clutter workspace eliminates competing visual 
   stimuli that usually cause cognitive fatigue. Additionally, the task 
   demands are crystal clear, reducing the "initiation paralysis" common in 
   schoolwork.

C. Physical/Tactile Feedback:
   The draggable shape elements mimic tangible blocks. Having clear physical-like 
   targets (glowing transparent stencils) provides visual anchoring that guides 
   motor control and sustained spatial attention.


2. NON-INVASIVE BEHAVIORAL SAMPLING (HOW IT GATHERS & UTILIZES DATA)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rather than utilizing artificial, stress-inducing assessments, the system 
collects clinical-grade interaction telemetry silently during standard, joyful play:

- Physical Screen Swipes & Micro-Press events: Measures drag distances, hover 
  densities, and fine-motor tremor or erratic drift (logPress).
- Accurate Durations: Tracks times elapsed from initial touch of a shape to a 
  satisfactory snap, or to an incorrect release.
- Chronological Match Streams: Logs the exact path of shapes the child attempts, 
  success distributions, and chronological time logs.

HOW THE DATA IS INVALUABLE FOR THE CHILD:
1. Adapting Learning Rhythms: If telemetry shows a sudden drop-off in Speed 
   and an increase in Failed Matches after 2 minutes of play, this is a clear 
   indicator of attention fatigue. Teachers or parents can use this to schedule 
   preventative micro-breaks in their actual homework schedules.
2. Motor Coordination Screening: Tracking average selection-to-match gaps 
   and drift distances lets specialists monitor fine-motor trajectory control, 
   acting as a safe, continuous metric for physical progression.
3. Task Patience & Self-Regulation Tracking: Analyzing the ratio of correct 
   snaps to rapid, erratic trial-and-error attempts offers direct visibility 
   into impulse control and cognitive persistence.


3. C++ BACKEND ANALYTICAL METRICS SPECIFICTION & MATHEMATICAL DERIVATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
All data points are offloaded from the QML rendering thread and processed in 
the C++ TelemetryBackend engine (telemetrybackend.cpp) to guarantee high-precision 
indexing and ensure zero UI stutter.

The three primary high-level indices are derived as follows:

A. PROBLEM SOLVING INDEX (COGNITIVE PERSISTENCE / TASK STYLE)
   - Focus: Measures precision, planning, and task accuracy. Helps flag 
     high impulsivity vs. mindful planning.
   - Equation:
     Let C = Total Correct Match Successes.
     Let F = Total Failed Match Attempts.
     Let T = Total Match Attempts = C + F.

     If T > 0:
         Raw_Index = (C / T) * 100.0 - (F * 2.5)
         Problem_Solving_Index = Clamp(Raw_Index, 10.0, 100.0)
     Otherwise:
         Problem_Solving_Index = 100.0

   - ADHD Interpretation: Children who act very impulsively will have clean, 
     rapid dragging but a high rate of failures (F), resulting in a lower 
     solving index. Over time, as self-regulation strategies take hold, F 
     decreases, or C/T improves, causing this index to climb towards 100%.

B. SPEED SCORE (PROCESSING SPEED & MOTOR CONVERGENCY)
   - Focus: Evaluates the rate of mental translation and spatial action.
   - Equation:
     Let S_correct = Cumulative sum of durations (in milliseconds) for all C.
     Let Average_Correct_Time_Sec = (S_correct / C) / 1000.0 (if C > 0; else 5.0)

     Raw_Score = 100.0 - (Average_Correct_Time_Sec * 6.5)
     Speed_Score = Clamp(Raw_Score, 15.0, 100.0)

   - ADHD Interpretation: Very low speeds (prolonged times) may reflect lapses in 
     vigilance, inattentive drifting, or physical hesitation. Overly high speeds 
     coupled with high failure rates describe hyperactive-impulsive approaches.

C. CREATIVE INDEX (EXPLORATION DENSITY & CURIOSITY KINETICS)
   - Focus: Maps the child's spatial exploration behavior, tactile curiosity, 
     and gameplay playfulness.
   - Equation:
     Let N_press = The total count of screen click/press/drag events logged.
     Let F = Total Failed Match attempts.

     Raw_Score = 50.0 + (N_press * 2.5) - (F * 1.5)
     Creative_Index = Clamp(Raw_Score, 20.0, 100.0)

   - ADHD Interpretation: This index tracks interaction energy. Instead of 
     punishing errors, it rewards active spatial exploration (N_press). Active, 
     curious touch is a healthy sign of sensory-motor experimentation, balanced 
     by a mild penalty for uncalculated, repetitive fails out of frustration.
